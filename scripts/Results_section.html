Results

Overview and Uncertainty

Presence-absence point count data on 12 forest bird species on Montserrat was sampled over 13 years between 2011 and 2024. Consequently, we analysed 39120 observations from 3260 surveys using dynamic occupancy models that correct for imperfect detection, calculate colonisation and extinction probability and provide the possibility to estimate yearly occupancy. We found evidence for elevational range changes in 5 out of the 12 analysed species (Figure 2, Table 3), with 3 species (Bridled Quail-Dove, Scaly-Breasted Thrasher and Brown Trembler) best explained by the ‘contraction’ model where extinction depended on elevation, 1 species (Purple-Throated Carib) where extinction was explained by elevation (‘expansion’ model), and 1 species (Montserrat Oriole) where both colonisation and extinction were explained by elevation (‘shift’ model). For another 4 species (Banaquit, Forest Thrush, Pearly-Eyed Thrasher and Scaly-Naped Pigeon) the most parsimonious model was the ‘constant’ model where both colonisation and extinction were modeled with intercept only, and for 3 species (Antillean Crested Hummingbird, Caribbean Eleania and Green-Throated Carib) both extinction and colonisation were best explained by year (‘year_num’ model). The GOF test (n = 1000 simulations, Table SUMMARY TABLE) indicated Chisquare p-values ranging from 0.428 to 0.999 . C-hat values for all models laid below 1, but underdispersion was negligible (c-hat > 0.9, SOURCE) for 8 . Thus, 4 models showed c-hat values substantially lower than 1, potentially indicating a lack-of-fit that may affect the validity of the models for inference. Moreover, model selection uncertainty occurs in the most parsimonious models for 5 out of 12 analysed species including 2 (Bridled-Quail Dove and Purple-Throated Carib) out of 5 models which suggest elevational range changes. For these models ΔAIC lies within 2 AIC units and therefore the alternative models are not clearly distinguishable from the most parsimonious one (Burnham and Anderson, 2002).

Dynamics of colonisation and extinction along elevation

For the Montserrat Oriole colonisation increased with elevation from 0.1256 to 0.9864 and extinction decreased with elevation from 0.1515 to 0.0039 showing effect sizes of 1.4823 (0.95 CI: 0.5027, 2.4619) and -0.9113 (0.95 CI: -1.5893, -0.2332), respectively. Notably, the steepness of both colonisation and extinction slopes was highest for low elevations but flattening towards higher elevations. The ‘expansion’ model for the Purple-Throated Carib suggested a positive colonisation-elevation relation of 1.008 (0.95 CI: 0.0828 , 1.9331) leading to values between 0.3411 and 0.9727. The models for the Brown Trembler and Bridled Quail-Dove indicated decreasing extinction along elevation (ranging from 0.3796 - 0.0001 and 0.1666 - 0.0022) but small effect sizes of -2.1204 (0.95 CI: -2.993, -1.2479), and -1.0714 (0.95 CI: -1.8875, -0.2554), respectively. Again, extinction flattened considerably towards higher elevations. Contrastingly, for the Scaly-Breasted Thrasher extinction generally increased with elevation spanning from 0 to 0.1657 and the estimated effect size was 2.7481 (0.95 CI: 0.9025, 4.5936). All effect sizes were reported on the logit-scale.

5 species showed very high mean occupancies mainly between 0.95 and 1 and narrow widths of the 0.95 confidence interval: The occupancy of the Pearly-Eyed Thrasher and Bananaquit were almost every year 1 (with means of 0.9991, respectively). Slightly lower occupancies were reported for the Scaly-Breasted Thrasher, Forest Thrush and Scaly-Naped Pigeon with means of 0.9844, 0.9932 and 0.9759, respectively. For 4 species mean occupancy lay between 0.85 and 0.95 but showed higher uncertainties. Namely these species were the Montserrat Oriole (0.8292), the Brown Trembler (0.9092), the Purple-Throated Carib (0.8883) and Antillean Crested Hummingbird (0.9407). The remaining 3 species, the Bridled Quail-Dove, Green-Throated Carib and Caribbean Elaenia, showed remarkable yearly variation and low precision in occupancy estimates resulting in means of 0.8720, 0.4755, 0.3325, respectively.

During the study period from 2011 to 2023, 8 species showed no clear trend in occupancy. But remarkable trends were observed in 4 species: Occupancies increased for the Montserrat Oriole by 14.04 %, and the Purple-Throated Carib by 26.24 %, whereas occupancies of the Green-Throated Carib dropped by 80.75 %, and Caribbean Elaenia by 97.26 %.
